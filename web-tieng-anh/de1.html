Three travellers talking about their holidays.

Max

I travelled to Bangkok in Thailand with some friends. The flight was long, but we found a cheap deal online. But then, it took two hours by bus to get to the hotel - I didn't enjoy that! The hotel was great though, with a huge swimming pool, and the best thing was, we spent almost nothing once we arrived! We mostly ate delicious, cheap street food, and stayed in the air-conditioned rooms at the hotel to escape the heat at night, but it was hard to sleep with such high temperatures, even with the air conditioning on.

Felipe

I went to a small town in southern France for two weeks with my family. The people who worked there were wonderful - they were so friendly and helpful, and they taught me some French! I wasn't so sure about the hotel restaurant - the desserts were fine, but the main courses weren't very good. It was much better in the town, where there were lots of lovely restaurants and cafes. It was so hot that we spent most of our time in the hotel swimming pool. I spent ages reading my book by the pool, and it was so relaxing.

Nikhil

My family and I visited some relatives in India, which was fantastic. I couldn't believe the differences in what people ate from place to place - we tried so many interesting dishes! It was good to be with the family, because there was always someone to talk to, or to do things with. The local buses were really cheap and comfortable, but they were often late. So we usually took taxis, which were more expensive, but much faster. We often spent the mornings by the beach, and in the afternoons we went for walks in the hills.

Better ways to travel

Travelling by car is fast and comfortable, but not **good** for the world around us. In fact, it makes a lot of pollution. Many countries are trying to find **better** ways for people to travel. One possibility is to **share** car journeys. For example, if you have neighbours who need to travel to the same **place** as you, you could go together. This means there would be fewer cars **making** the same journey. Another good idea is to use public transport more often, for example, travel by bus, tram or train, **especially** in a town or city. You can also walk or cycle more often, **instead** of driving. You'll get fit and **save** money too!
